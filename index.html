Welcome

The Massachusetts Computer Science Olympiad is a high-school programming competition hosted by students at Brookline High. We create coding questions focusing on clever techniques, and students of all programming backgrounds are welcome to participate!

FAQ

Contact questions@macso.org for any questions.

Who can participate in the competition?
The competition is open to high school students of all programming backgrounds. Whether you are a beginner or an advanced coder, you are welcome to participate!
How does the competition work?
The competition has two parts: An online qualification stage (3 hour time limit), and in-person finals. The qualification stage consists of three coding problems and the top 50 qualify for finals.
What is the scoring system like?
The scoring is based on the number of total points from 3 problems with 20 test cases each. There are 3 thresholds for how long the code takes to run. If you pass the first threshold, you get 3 points, 4 points for the second threshold, and 5 points for the third (per test case). There are 300 points maxiumum with 100 points per problem.
When and where is the competition?"
The competition will be hosted at the Brookline Teen Center on October 19th, 2024. The in person competition is three hours with free pizza, prizes, and activities
How can I register for the competition?
You can register for the competition by clicking on the Register Here button at the top of the page. It will take you to the registration form where you can fill in your details.

Meet the Team

We are a group of students at Brookline High School passionate about computer science. Our goal is for aspiring programmers in Massachusetts to experience the field of computer science beyond the school curriculum. Near Brookline, there are many prestigious universities such as Boston College, Boston University, MIT, and Harvard.
